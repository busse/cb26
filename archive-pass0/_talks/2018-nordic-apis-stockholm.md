Delivered keynote using my signature **"APIs for Lemonade Stands"** workshop based on a 1979 Apple II game. This approach makes API-first design principles accessible and memorable.

## Key Topics

- **API-first/Design-first/Contract-first approaches** – Understanding the spectrum of API design methodologies
- **Legacy application redesign methodology** – A practical two-step approach
- **Building empathy for end users** – Why developers need to "get out of the building"
- **RESTful API design** – Best practices for resource-oriented design
- **Developer experience (DX)** – Making APIs a joy to use

## The Two-Step Methodology

The talk introduced a practical methodology for redesigning legacy applications:

1. **First, concern yourself with resources** – Identify the domain entities and their relationships
2. **Then, concern yourself with payloads/requests/responses** – Design the representations and interactions

## Key Insight

> "Get out of the building and sit with end users."

Understanding how people actually use your systems is the foundation of good API design. The Lemonade Stand exercise makes this tangible by forcing participants to think about the business domain before jumping to technical implementation.
