The missing window panes tell you everything. Not the broken ones — the *missing* ones. Someone removed them deliberately. Whether for salvage, ventilation, or simple entropy, the absence is more instructive than the damage.

This factory in Manchester is in the intermediate state that most people find ugly and urbanists find fascinating. It is no longer what it was built to be. It is not yet what it will become. It exists in the gap between destruction and creation — Shiva's territory.

The Shaiva tradition holds that destruction is not the opposite of creation but its prerequisite. The *tandava* consumes what is no longer vital so that something new can emerge. This building is mid-dance. The commerce that filled it has moved on. The infrastructure persists, waiting. The cultural question — do we tear it down, preserve it, or transform it? — remains unanswered.

What catches my eye is the one intact window in the upper right. It's holding on, still doing its job, still keeping weather out of a room that nobody uses. There is something Stoic in that. It controls what it can control. It keeps the rain out. It does not concern itself with the missing panes beside it.
